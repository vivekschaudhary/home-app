# Changelog

User-visible changes. One entry per shipped bet (not per PR — PRs accumulate, finalize when brief ships).

Format based on [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/).

## [Unreleased]

### Added
- **Identity & MFA onboarding (WLT-1, story WLT-6):** create an account with email + password and a **mandatory passkey** enrolled in the same flow; sign in with a passkey challenge. Sessions persist across reloads; the second factor is enforced server-side. Unsupported browsers get an honest block. _(Supabase Auth + a custom WebAuthn 2FA layer — architecture ADR-001.)_
- **Connect your bank (WLT-2, story WLT-9):** link a bank account through Plaid from an explicit consent screen, then see your real transactions imported (last 90 days) within seconds; view connected accounts with balances and sync status, and disconnect anytime (history kept, updates stop). Bank credentials are never seen or stored; access tokens live encrypted in Supabase Vault, never in app tables. _(Provider-neutral `@wealth/aggregation` pipeline — Plaid via ADR-002, swappable by design.)_
- **Tell us what you want (WLT-3, story WLT-11):** right after sign-in — before connecting anything — declare what you'd like help with across six plain, first-person choices (worried about overspending, saving for something, making sense of it all, and more). Your intent is saved and used to set up the right plan; "not sure yet" lets you look around with no dead-end. _(Intent-first, user-first onboarding; intents/goals owner-scoped with same-user enforcement at the DB.)_
- **Your full history, kept fresh (WLT-2, story WLT-10):** connecting a bank now imports up to **24 months** of history (not just the recent window), and an honest "Importing your history…" state stays until it's actually all in — even if you leave and come back. After that, new transactions and balance changes sync automatically. _(Plaid `days_requested:730` + a signature-verified webhook with a scheduled fallback; the "done" signal is real sync stabilization, not a timer.)_
- **Your plan starts working (WLT-4, story WLT-12):** tell us what you want and connect your bank, and the platform turns it into a real plan — your actual net worth ("Your money, right now": assets, debts, the number) with one clear next move: **set your target**, one tap on a suggested figure or your own. From then on it reads "Running — tracking toward your target," and it's still there every time you come back. Until you connect, it says so honestly — your plan is ready, never a made-up number. _(The workflow engine: goal → archetype → personalization on real balances → an immutable action record; the first turn of the product's core loop.)_
- **The loop measures itself (WLT-5, story WLT-13 — internal):** an internal, admin-only instrument panel computes **TTFV** (signup → first action, p80 vs the 3-min target, with split times), **WAWU** (the weekly north star), and **stage-by-stage funnel conversion** from the events the loop already emits — recorded as dated snapshots for the review cadence. No user-facing change; this is the instrumentation that makes the foundational hypothesis falsifiable. _(Read-only Postgres views, privilege-revoked; an AAL2 + allow-list page that 404s for everyone else; aggregates only, no PII. **Completes the MVP bet portfolio.**)_

- **Forgot your password (WLT-1, story WLT-14):** if you can't sign in, "Forgot password?" sends a reset link to your email; follow it to set a new password and you're back. For your safety, you'll still use your passkey to finish signing in — a reset only restores the password. The page never reveals whether an email has an account. _(Supabase Auth recovery; anti-enumeration + rate-limited; recovers the first factor only.)_
- **Your money since last time (WLT-15, story WLT-16) — _shipped behind a flag_:** the dashboard gains a recurring "Since last time" recap — how your net worth **moved** since the last few days, your **progress toward the target** you set (the plan now actually tracks instead of just sitting there), and **one** clear next move (adjust your target, or aim higher) you can take again each week. Cold-start is honest — "we've started watching your money; you'll see how it's moving in a few days" — never a made-up number. Currently dark behind `RECAP_ENABLED` until the daily net-worth sampler has a cycle of history to anchor movement. _(A daily net-worth snapshot series + a repeatable, weekly-idempotent action record; the first slice of the engagement bet — spending and anomalies follow.)_
- **Where your money went (WLT-15, story WLT-17) — _shipped behind the same flag_:** the recap now also shows **how much you spent this week vs. last** and your **top spending categories** — the "where did it go" breakdown, in plain words ("Spent $420 this week · $120 more than last week"), never scolding. First week shows this week only until there's history to compare. _(A read over your existing transactions — amounts + categories only, no merchant names; the recap's second signal.)_
- **Something worth your attention (WLT-15, story WLT-18) — _completes the recap_:** the recap now flags **"Worth a look"** — a larger-than-usual charge (vs. your own typical spend), a bill that looks due soon, or an account running low — with one move: **Review it** or **Dismiss**. It's deliberately quiet and **high-precision** (conservative rules, always dismissible, never alarming, never "fraud") — a wrong alert would be worse than none. _(Daily rules-based scan over your own data — amounts/categories/dates only, no merchant names. The fourth and final signal; the **"Your money since last time" engagement bet is complete** — all four signals live behind `RECAP_ENABLED`.)_
- **Budget & Spending (WLT-21, story WLT-21-1):** the first section of the new app — set a **monthly budget per category** and see, at a glance, a **recommended** limit drawn from your **own spending history**, what you've **spent this month so far**, and whether you're **under or over**. Set a budget as a dollar amount or a percent of your typical monthly spending; add any bank category to budget — even one you haven't spent in yet. Cold-start is honest — a recommendation appears only once there's about a month of history, never a made-up number — and there's nothing to budget until you connect an account. **You can also expand any category to a 12-month chart of its spending** (WLT-21-2) — your own rhythm across the year, with this month marked as still in progress. _(A per-category table over your real transactions — amounts/categories only, no merchant names; an owner-only `budgets` table; recommendations + the year chart computed from your trailing months, no income data used; the chart is hand-drawn SVG — no charting dependency.)_
- **One home for everything (WLT-19, story WLT-20):** the app now lives in a single, full-screen, responsive shell with a persistent left navigation — **Dashboard · Budget & Spending · Goals · Debt payoff · Investments · Subscriptions · Accounts** — that works cleanly on phone (a tap-to-open drawer), tablet, and desktop (a fixed sidebar). Your account menu (Security · Sign out) sits at the foot of the nav. Dashboard and Accounts are wired in; the five not-yet-built sections show an honest **"Coming soon"** — never fake data. Every screen stays behind your second factor; the old `/settings/accounts` link still works (it now lands on Accounts). _(A Next.js `(app)` route group with one shared layout that enforces AAL2 once and renders the nav from a single config — the frame this week's section features mount into; built on Headless UI + Tailwind.)_

- **See what's behind a category's number (WLT-22, story WLT-22-1):** on Budget & Spending, tap a category's **"this month so far"** amount to reveal the **real transactions** behind it — date · merchant · amount, newest first — and the panel's **Total equals that number exactly**, so you can finally verify _"what got counted as rent."_ Line items load on demand, an empty category says so plainly, and a failed load shows a calm "try again" instead of a blank panel — your own data only, behind your second factor. _(A read over existing owner-scoped transactions — no schema, no category model; forward-compatible with WLT-22-2's user-owned categories. Proven owner-isolated by a real-path session→RLS→render E2E incl. a cross-tenant negative case.)_
- **Remember a merchant (WLT-22, story WLT-22-3) — _completes Budget transparency + category correction_:** when you fix a transaction's category, you can now check **"Always categorize [merchant] this way"** — and every transaction from that merchant, **past and future**, takes that category automatically (it tells you how many it updated). A recurring mis-tag gets corrected **once** instead of every month, and new transactions from that merchant arrive already sorted. Your own one-off corrections always win — a remembered rule never overrides a category you set by hand. _(A `category_rules` merchant→category mapping that writes saved per-transaction assignments — backfilled on create, applied at sync — read through the same one resolver so budget/recap/anomalies stay in agreement; proven by an RLS suite + a real-path E2E covering backfill, the user-override precedence, future-sync auto-categorization, and cross-tenant isolation. The **"verify → correct → remember" bet is complete.**)_
- **Fix a miscategorized transaction — and it sticks (WLT-22, story WLT-22-2):** from that same drill-down, **move a transaction to the right category**, or **create your own category** to split a lumped group (pull "Rent" out of "Rent & Utilities"). The correction is **saved as yours** — it survives your bank's re-syncs — and the moment you make it the numbers reconcile: the category it left drops, the one it joins rises, and your budget, the recap, and anomalies all reflect **your** category, not the provider's guess. Your bank's category stays only as the starting suggestion for anything you haven't touched. _(A new owner-scoped saved-category model — categories + per-transaction assignments keyed to a stable transaction identity — read through one shared resolver so every surface agrees; manual correction this slice, "remember this merchant" rules next. Proven by an RLS suite + a real-path E2E covering re-sync survival and cross-tenant isolation.)_
- **All your transactions, in one place (WLT-23 — bet COMPLETE: WLT-23-1 + WLT-23-2 + WLT-23-3):** a new **Transactions** tab in the left nav opens a single, searchable list of **everything across your accounts**, newest first — date · merchant · amount · category · account. **Search** by merchant or description, **filter by account or category**, and **load more** to page back through a full two years of history without it ever feeling slow. Income and refunds show as credits (with a +), pending charges are flagged, and each transaction's **category matches your budget** — including any you've corrected. And you can **fix a category right from the list** — tap a transaction's category to move it, create a new category, or "always categorize this merchant this way," the same correction you know from the budget, in a tidy popover. It's a clean read of your real activity — your own data, behind your second factor; amounts/dates aren't editable (your bank owns the entries). _(An owner-scoped, keyset-paginated read over existing transactions — no new schema — reusing the WLT-22 saved-category resolver + `CategoryPicker` so the category never disagrees with the budget; the category filter resolves per-row through a bounded, injection-safe keyset scan. Proven owner-isolated by real-path session→RLS→render E2Es covering filtered page-boundary pagination + cross-tenant negatives; recategorize reuses WLT-22's proven write path. **The Transactions bet is complete — see · find · correct.**)_

### Changed
- **Budget detail now opens in a popover (WLT-21/WLT-22 polish):** tapping a category's **"this month so far"** amount (its line items) or **"View the year"** (the 12-month chart) now opens a clean **popover** anchored to the row, instead of expanding a panel inside the table that pushed the other rows around. The table stays put; everything else — verifying the number, correcting a category, "remember this merchant" — works exactly as before. _(Headless UI popovers; one open at a time.)_

### Fixed
- **"Always categorize this merchant" now updates transactions you'd previously categorized by hand (FIX-2026-06-20):** when you pick a category for a merchant and check "always categorize this way," it now applies to **all** of that merchant's transactions — including ones you'd individually recategorized before (e.g. older entries from a prior year) and the one you're editing. Before, those kept their old category and the change appeared to "not stick." Your scattered one-off choices for that merchant are superseded by the explicit "always" decision; a later one-off recategorization (without "remember") still stays put. _(The merchant rule, on an explicit remember, now overrides the user's own prior per-transaction assignments; automatic re-application during background sync still leaves your manual one-offs untouched.)_
- **"Remember this merchant" now catches the same store's name variants (INC-2026-06-19):** when you tell us to always categorize a merchant a certain way, new transactions from that merchant are now matched even when your bank reports the name slightly differently — e.g. **Walmart**, **Walmart.com**, **WAL-MART**, and **Walmart Supercenter #1234** are all treated as the same merchant (while genuinely different ones like **Walmart Pharmacy** stay separate). Previously a new transaction under a different spelling slipped through as the bank's default category. Your existing rules pick this up automatically on the next sync — no action needed. _(Two layers: it now matches primarily on the **provider's stable merchant id** — the bank/Plaid's own canonical identity for the merchant, which holds across name variants — and falls back to a more robust normalized name key (store numbers / ".com" / format words normalized away) when no id is present. Applied to both new and pre-existing rules, with deterministic newest-wins handling when older rules collapse together. This also lets a rule match a transaction the bank leaves with **no merchant name** as long as it has a merchant id; only transactions with neither stay unmatched. Proven end to end by a real-path "same merchant id, different/blank name → auto-categorizes, second user isolated" test.)_ if your account has an authenticator app set up, resetting your password used to fail with an opaque error — because a reset isn't allowed to skip your second factor. Now the reset page asks for your **authenticator code** to confirm it's you, then sets your new password. Reused (or otherwise unacceptable) passwords also get a clear inline reason instead of a generic failure. _(Adds the second-factor step to recovery — AAL1→AAL2 — and discriminated `updateUser` errors; `@vc1023/passkey-2fa@0.4.2`.)_
- **Clearer sign-up errors (SUP-8):** if you hit the rate limit (or pick a password the system can't accept) while creating an account, you now get a specific, actionable message instead of a generic "something went wrong." _(Discriminated `signUp` errors — kept deliberately quiet on whether an email is already registered, to avoid leaking who has an account; `@vc1023/passkey-2fa@0.4.3`.)_
